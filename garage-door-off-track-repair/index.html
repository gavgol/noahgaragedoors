Do Not Attempt to Force the Door

A door that's off its tracks has lost the mechanical support that controls its movement. Operating it — manually or with the opener — can cause it to fall suddenly. This is how injuries happen. The right move is to leave it where it is and call us.

What Causes a Garage Door to Go Off Track?

📿
Broken CableWhen a lifting cable snaps, one side of the door drops, pulling the rollers off the track on that side.
🔩
Worn or Broken RollersOld plastic rollers crack and break. Metal rollers wear down. When they fail, the door jumps the track.
🚗
Vehicle ImpactA vehicle backing into the door is one of the most common causes. Even a minor bump can knock rollers out of alignment.
🔄
Broken SpringA failed spring creates uneven tension, causing the door to tilt and one side to slip off its track.
📐
Bent or Damaged TrackA track bent by impact or improper installation creates a gap the rollers can't pass through.
⚙️
Loose HardwareTrack mounting brackets loosen over time. Eventually the track shifts enough that rollers can't stay seated.

Our Off-Track Repair Process

01

Full Inspection

We assess the door, tracks, rollers, cables, and springs to identify the root cause — not just put it back on and hope for the best.

02

Root Cause Repair

We fix what caused the problem — whether it's a broken cable, failed roller, bent track, or failed spring. Skipping this step means the door goes off-track again.

03

Track Realignment

We carefully reseat the rollers, realign the tracks to manufacturer spec, and tighten all mounting hardware securely.

04

Safety Test & Balance Check

We run the door through full open/close cycles, test the auto-reverse safety, and verify proper balance before leaving.

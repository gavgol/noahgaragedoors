Signs Your Garage Door Spring Is Broken

Springs break suddenly, often with a loud bang that sounds like a firecracker going off in your garage. Here's what to look for:

💥
Loud BangA sudden loud bang from the garage, that's the spring snapping under tension.
🚫
Door Won't OpenOpener runs but the door only lifts a few inches, the motor can't carry the full weight alone.
🔍
Visible Gap in SpringA separated or stretched coil visible in the torsion spring above the door.
⚠️
Door Feels Extremely HeavyIf the door seems to weigh 200+ lbs when you lift it manually, a spring has failed.
📉
Door Falls Fast When ClosingAn unbalanced door slams down rather than lowering smoothly, spring tension is gone.
🪝
Cables Are SlackLoose or hanging cables at the bottom corners mean the spring is no longer providing tension.

Why Garage Door Springs Break in San Diego

Every torsion spring is rated for a set number of cycles, usually 10,000 to 20,000 open-and-close cycles for a standard builder-grade spring. For a family that uses the garage door four times a day, that works out to roughly 7 to 14 years. San Diego's environment can shorten that in ways most homeowners never see coming.

Our climate is mild, but homes near the coast in Pacific Beach, Ocean Beach, Mission Hills, and Point Loma sit in salty, humid ocean air that accelerates metal corrosion. Inland areas like El Cajon, Santee, and Escondido see bigger temperature swings, and the constant expansion and contraction gradually weakens the coil. In more temperate neighborhoods like North Park, Normal Heights, and Hillcrest, springs simply wear out from regular use.

Signs a spring is getting close to the end of its life:

  • The door feels unusually heavy when you lift it manually.
  • The opener strains or struggles more than it used to.
  • You can see gaps or separations forming in the spring coil.
  • Rust or corrosion is building up on the spring surface.
  • The door opens unevenly and sags on one side.

If you want the full breakdown, our guides on the signs of a broken garage door spring and how long garage door springs last go deeper on what to watch for.

Torsion vs. Extension Springs, What's the Difference?

🔄

Torsion Springs

Mounted horizontally above the door on a steel shaft. Most San Diego homes have this type. They twist (torque) to store energy as the door closes and release it when opening.

  • Safer, contained when they break
  • Longer lifespan (10,000–30,000 cycles)
  • Better balance and smoother operation
↔️

Extension Springs

Run along both sides of the door horizontally. Common in older or lower-clearance garages. They stretch to store energy and can travel long distances if they break without a safety cable.

  • Require safety cables to be safe
  • Shorter lifespan (7,500–10,000 cycles)
  • Lower cost but more maintenance
⚠️

Why DIY Spring Repair Is Dangerous

Garage door springs are under 200–300 lbs of stored tension. A spring that snaps while you're working on it can cause serious injury or death. This is consistently ranked one of the most dangerous home repair jobs.

Our technicians are trained specifically in spring mechanics, use proper winding bars and safety equipment, and know exactly how many turns of tension your specific door requires. For your safety, call us.

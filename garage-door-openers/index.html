Common Opener Problems We Fix

Our technicians diagnose and repair all opener issues on-site. Here's what we see most often in San Diego:

🔇
Motor Runs, Door Doesn't MoveUsually stripped gears or a broken drive gear inside the opener — a common repair on older units.
📡
Remote Won't WorkCould be dead batteries, a de-synced remote, or a failed receiver board. We test and reprogram or replace.
🔄
Door Reverses Before ClosingMisaligned safety sensors or limit switch setting. Sensors need cleaning or realignment.
💡
Logic Board FailureThe "brain" of the opener can fail due to power surges. We replace logic boards on most brands.
📳
Noisy OperationGrinding or rattling can be worn rollers, loose hardware, or a chain/belt that needs adjustment.
⚡
No Power / Won't Turn OnCould be a tripped breaker, blown capacitor, or damaged outlet. We trace the issue to the source.

Repair or Replace? Here's How We Decide

✅ Repair When:

  • • Opener is under 8 years old
  • • Single component failure (gear, sensor, board)
  • • Repair cost is under 50% of replacement
  • • Unit is still efficient and quiet

🔄 Replace When:

  • • Opener is 10+ years old
  • • Repeated repairs in the last year
  • • Motor is failing or very noisy
  • • You want smart home integration

Our technicians give honest recommendations — we'll tell you if repair isn't worth it and show you a replacement that fits your budget.

Why Garage Door Springs Break in San Diego

Every torsion spring is rated for a specific number of cycles — typically 10,000 to 20,000 open-and-close cycles for standard springs. For a family using their garage door four times a day, that translates to roughly 7 to 14 years of life. But San Diego's unique environment can shorten that lifespan in ways most homeowners don't expect.

While our climate is mild, homes near the coast — in neighborhoods like Pacific Beach, Ocean Beach, Mission Hills, and Point Loma — deal with salty, humid ocean air that accelerates metal corrosion. Inland areas like El Cajon, Santee, and Escondido experience greater temperature swings that cause metal to expand and contract, gradually weakening the spring's coil structure. Even in more temperate neighborhoods like North Park, Normal Heights, and Hillcrest, springs simply wear out with regular use over time.

Common signs your spring is close to failing include:

  • The door feels unusually heavy when you try to lift it manually
  • The opener struggles or strains more than usual
  • Visible gaps or separations in the spring coil
  • Rust or corrosion forming on the spring surface
  • The door opens unevenly, sagging on one side

The Danger of DIY Spring Replacement

Look, we're not just saying this to get your business: replacing a garage door spring yourself is genuinely dangerous. It's one of the leading causes of serious DIY home repair injuries. Torsion springs are wound under extreme tension — a fully loaded spring stores enough energy to cause severe lacerations, broken bones, or worse if it slips or releases suddenly during installation or removal.

Professional technicians use specialized winding bars, proper safety gear, and years of training to handle springs safely. The tools sold at hardware stores are inadequate substitutes. Even experienced handymen who work on everything else in their homes draw the line at garage door springs for good reason.

Save the DIY energy for painting your fence. Call us for the springs.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does a garage door spring replacement take?

Most spring replacements take 45 minutes to 90 minutes from the time the technician arrives. If only one spring needs replacement and no other hardware issues are found, you could be back to your normal routine in under an hour. We don't rush, but we don't waste your time either.

Can I still use my garage door after a spring breaks?

We strongly advise against it. Without a functioning spring, the full weight of the door (often 150–400 lbs) falls on the opener motor, which can burn it out quickly. More importantly, a door without spring tension is unpredictable and can drop suddenly, posing a serious safety risk to anyone nearby. Disengage the automatic opener and leave the door closed until a technician arrives.

Should I replace one spring or both?

If your door uses two torsion springs, we recommend replacing both at the same time. Both springs were installed simultaneously and have the same wear history. When one breaks, the other is typically within a few months of failure. Replacing both in one visit saves you a second labor charge and keeps you from being stranded again soon.

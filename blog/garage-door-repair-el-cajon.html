Why El Cajon Homes Have Unique Garage Door Needs

El Cajon's inland location means its homes deal with a climate that's noticeably different from coastal San Diego. Summer temperatures regularly hit 90–105°F, and the temperature swings between day and night are more extreme than in beach communities. This puts extra stress on torsion springs, which expand and contract with temperature changes, accelerating metal fatigue. El Cajon homes, particularly those in Fletcher Hills, Rancho San Diego, Granite Hills, and Bostonia, tend to see higher spring failure rates than coastal neighborhoods.

Additionally, many El Cajon homes were built in the 1970s through 1990s, meaning the original garage door hardware is at or past its expected lifespan. If your home still has the original springs and opener from that era, you're running borrowed time.

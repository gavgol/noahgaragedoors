Spring Replacement: The Most Common Repair

Spring replacement is the single most common garage door service call in San Diego. Torsion springs are rated for 10,000–15,000 cycles, which translates to roughly 7–12 years of daily use. When one breaks, the door typically won't open at all. Prices reflect the spring hardware itself, labor, balance testing, and safety check. We recommend replacing both springs simultaneously if your door uses two. It saves a second labor charge when the other side fails, and it will.

What Determines the Final Price?

Several factors affect where in the range your job lands:

  • Door size and weight: Heavy double car doors and specialty doors require heavier hardware and more labor.
  • Spring type: Torsion springs (most common in SD homes built after 1990) cost more than extension springs but last longer.
  • Brand and model of opener: Premium smart opener units cost more than basic chain-drive models.
  • Multiple issues in one visit: If we find additional problems during the repair (e.g., worn cables + off-track + bad rollers), fixing them all together is cheaper than separate visits.
  • Time of day: Standard hours vs. after-hours emergency service.

Red Flags When Getting a Quote

San Diego has its share of fly-by-night garage door operations. Watch out for:

  • An $89 "special" that balloons to $600 on site. A low advertised price gets you on the hook, then they quote extreme prices for springs or parts once they're there.
  • Quotes without seeing the door. No honest company gives you a fixed price without at least knowing your door type, size, and what failed.
  • Pressure to replace more than needed. A reputable tech tells you what actually needs fixing, not what generates the most revenue.
  • No license or insurance. Ask directly. If they dodge the question, find someone else.

Should I repair or replace my garage door?

Repair if: the issue is isolated (spring, cable, opener, off-track) and the door panels are in good shape. Replace if: multiple panels are cracked or bent, the door is over 20 years old and has ongoing issues, or you want to improve curb appeal and energy efficiency.

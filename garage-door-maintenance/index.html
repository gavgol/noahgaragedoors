What's Included in Every Tune-Up

Our $49 intro special is a complete professional service, not a watered-down inspection. Here's exactly what we do:

Full Visual InspectionWe check all panels, springs, cables, tracks, rollers, hinges, and hardware for wear, damage, or rust.
Lubrication of All Moving PartsSprings, rollers, hinges, and tracks get professional-grade lubricant, not WD-40. This reduces friction, noise, and wear significantly.
Balance TestWe disconnect the opener and manually lift the door to the midpoint. A balanced door holds steady. An unbalanced door means springs are under- or over-tensioned.
Spring Tension Check & AdjustmentProper spring tension is critical for safe operation and for not overworking the opener motor. We adjust as needed.
Safety Auto-Reverse TestFederal law requires all openers to reverse when the door contacts an object. We test and calibrate this to UL 325 safety standards.
Hardware TighteningA garage door opens and closes ~1,500 times per year. Bolts and brackets work loose. We tighten everything securely.
Cable & Roller InspectionWe check cables for fraying and rollers for wear. Catching these early prevents a much more expensive breakdown.
Weatherstripping CheckWe check the bottom seal and side weatherstripping for gaps that let in dust, pests, or weather. San Diego sun makes these seals brittle over time.
Opener Force Setting CheckThe force settings decide how hard the opener pushes and pulls. Set too high and the door may not reverse on an obstruction. Set too low and it stops early. We adjust them to manufacturer spec.

Signs Your Garage Door Needs a Tune-Up

A door rarely fails without warning. It usually tells you first. If you notice any of these, book a service visit before it turns into a breakdown:

  • Unusual noises: squeaking, grinding, or scraping during operation.
  • Slow, jerky, or uneven movement, or a door that sags on one side.
  • The door reverses unexpectedly or hesitates before closing.
  • Visible rust on springs or cables, or fraying strands in a cable.
  • The door feels heavier than it used to when you lift it by hand.

Noise in particular is worth chasing down early. Our guide on why a garage door gets noisy explains which sounds are harmless and which ones are not, and how long garage door springs last covers what regular service actually buys you.

Longer System LifeFriction is what kills garage door hardware. Keeping it lubricated and balanced stretches the life of springs, rollers, and the opener motor.
Fewer Emergency CallsCatching a frayed cable or a cracked roller during a tune-up is far cheaper than dealing with it after it fails, often at the worst possible moment.

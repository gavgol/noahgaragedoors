What Do Garage Door Cables Do?

Cables are the unsung heroes of your garage door system. They work in tandem with the springs to lift and lower the door in a controlled, balanced way. Each cable attaches at the bottom corner of the door and runs up to either a pulley (extension spring system) or a cable drum on the shaft (torsion spring system). Without properly functioning cables, the spring tension has no way to translate into controlled door movement, and the door can fall suddenly.

🔄

Torsion System Cables

Wind onto cable drums attached to the torsion shaft. As the shaft rotates, cables wind or unwind to raise or lower the door evenly on both sides.

↕️

Extension System Cables

Run through pulleys along the horizontal tracks. Also includes a safety cable threaded through each extension spring to contain it if the spring snaps.

Signs of a Broken or Failing Cable

📐
Door Hangs at an AngleOne side of the door is lower than the other, a classic sign that one cable has snapped or come off the drum.
🔗
Slack or Loose CableVisible coils of cable hanging loosely near the bottom bracket or piled on the floor.
🧵
Fraying or Broken StrandsIndividual wire strands visible on the cable surface, a warning sign before complete failure.
💨
Door Falls Too FastWithout a cable providing resistance, the door drops under its own weight instead of lowering smoothly.
🚧
Door Off the TracksA broken cable often causes the door to go off-track on one side as that corner loses support.
📳
Grinding or Scraping NoiseA cable come off its drum or pulley can scrape against the track or hardware as the door moves.
⚠️

Do Not Attempt Cable Repair Yourself

Garage door cables are under the same tension as the springs, hundreds of pounds of stored energy. If the cable snaps while you're working on it, it can cause severe injury. Cables are also connected to springs and drums in ways that require specific tools and training to handle safely.

Our technicians carry the right gauge cables for your specific door weight and handle this repair in under an hour. Call us, it's not worth the risk.

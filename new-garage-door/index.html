Buying a New Garage Door, Start to Finish

Replacing a garage door is a bigger decision than a repair, so it helps to know the whole path before you call. Here is how a new door project actually runs with Noah Garage Doors. Call (619) 572-4266 to start at step one.

  1. 1Phone conversation. We ask what is happening with the current door, what you want out of the new one, and roughly what your budget looks like. You get a realistic price range on the call, before anyone drives out.
  2. 2On-site measure and free estimate. We measure the opening, headroom, sideroom, and backroom, check the existing tracks and opener, and look at how the door sits against the front of the house. Then you get an itemized written quote. No obligation.
  3. 3Pick the door. Material, panel style, window layout, insulation level, color, and hardware. We show you what works for your opening and your street, and we tell you where spending more actually pays off and where it does not.
  4. 4Order and schedule. Common steel doors in standard sizes move fastest. Custom wood, full-view glass, and non-standard openings are built to order, so they take longer. We confirm the lead time with the supplier and give you an install date rather than a guess.
  5. 5Install day. The old door and hardware come out, the new door goes in, and the system is balanced and tested before we leave. Most residential installs are a single-day job.
  6. 6Walkthrough and warranty. We run the door with you, hand over the remotes and keypad codes, show you the safety reverse, and explain what the manufacturer's warranty on the door covers.

Coastal Salt Air vs Inland Heat

San Diego is not one climate, it is two, and the right door in Point Loma is not always the right door in El Cajon. This is the single biggest thing homeowners get wrong when they shop by picture instead of by location.

🌊

Near the Coast

Ocean Beach, Point Loma, Pacific Beach, La Jolla, Del Mar, Encinitas, Carlsbad, Oceanside, Imperial Beach and Coronado all sit in salt air. Salt is hard on bare steel, fasteners, springs, rollers, and hinges, and a cheap door will show rust bleed at the seams and hardware long before it wears out mechanically.

  • • Aluminum and full-view aluminum frames resist corrosion best.
  • • If you want steel, ask for a heavier gauge with a factory baked finish, not a thin painted panel.
  • • Galvanized or coated hardware, springs, and rollers matter as much as the door itself.
  • • Cedar and redwood hold up better than most woods here, but they need a maintained finish.
☀️

Inland and East County

El Cajon, Santee, Alpine, Jamul, Poway, Escondido, San Marcos and Chula Vista's inland edge run much hotter. Here the enemy is heat and sun, not salt. A west or south facing garage can turn into an oven, and a thin uninsulated door does nothing to stop it.

  • • Insulated steel is the workhorse choice, better temperature control and a much quieter door.
  • • Lighter colors and quality finishes fade and chalk less under constant sun.
  • • If the garage doubles as a gym, office, or workshop, insulation is not optional.
  • • Living space above the garage is the clearest case for the highest R-value you can budget for.

What Actually Drives the Price

Two houses on the same block can get quotes that are a thousand dollars apart, and it is almost never random. These are the factors that move the number, roughly in order of impact.

MaterialThe largest single factor. Steel sits at the affordable end, custom wood and full-view glass sit at the top.
SizeA single 9x7 costs less than a double 16x7. Oversized or non-standard openings mean a made-to-order door.
InsulationSingle-layer, double-layer polystyrene, or triple-layer polyurethane. Each step up adds cost, quiet, and R-value.
Windows and glassWindow sections, obscure or tinted glass, and full-view panels all add to the panel cost.
Finish and colorStock colors are cheapest. Custom paint, woodgrain finishes, and stains cost more and add lead time.
Hardware and springsA heavier door needs correctly sized springs. Higher-cycle springs and quiet nylon rollers cost more up front and last longer.
Track and headroomLow-headroom, high-lift, or vertical-lift track setups need extra parts and labor compared to a standard lift.
Opener conditionA healthy opener can usually stay. If it is failing or underpowered for a heavier door, replacing it is a separate line item you approve first.

What Install Day Looks Like

Most single and double residential doors are a same-day job. Plan on the garage being open and unusable for a few hours, so move your cars out and clear about three feet of space along the walls and in front of the opening before we arrive.

The old door comes down first, panel by panel, along with the old track and springs. The new track and hardware go up, then the panels go in from the bottom section upward. The springs are wound and tuned to the exact weight of your new door, which is what makes it feel light by hand and easy on the opener. After that we reconnect the opener, reset its travel and force settings, align the safety sensors, and test the auto-reverse.

Custom wood, oversized doors, and full-view glass take longer, and openings that need framing or track modifications can add time. If anything unexpected shows up behind the old door, you hear about it and approve the cost before we continue.
